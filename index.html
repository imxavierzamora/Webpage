Vinyl: Community
Beyond the Algorithm

The first record Erica Royo ever heard was playing on a plastic portable turntable with an American flag painted on it. Carolyn Wysinger once walked into Down Home Music in El Cerrito and found what may have been her own aunt's 1950s gospel record, a family artifact she discovered in a bin.

Hitting the sweet spots of emotion, memory and recognition, the constant low-level sounds of a record fitting into a turntable have allowed vinyl to become more than a music medium.


They've become catalysts for human connection across generations and strangers alike.

Across conversations with shop owners, DJs, producers, vendors, and collectors throughout the Bay Area, a handful of threads kept surfacing.
